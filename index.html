Project 1: Time-Resolved Photoluminescence

In quantum mechanics, we describe the intensity of the light emission over time. Since "everything is a wave," we often model the decay of carriers using an exponential relationship. The intensity \( I(t) \) at any given time \( t \) can be expressed as:

$$ I(t) = I_0 e^{-t/\tau} $$

Where \( \tau \) represents the carrier lifetime. By using CW lasers and sophisticated timing electronics, we can extract these lifetimes to determine material purity.
